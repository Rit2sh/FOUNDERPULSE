Essay Founder Psychology
The Placement Dependency Trap Is Killing Indian Innovation — And Founders Must Break It
For decades, the highest aspiration for engineering graduates was a ₹30LPA package. That mindset, deeply entrenched in IIT culture and reinforced by anxious parents, has cost India an entire generation of potential founders.
"The best founders I've met from Tier-2 colleges didn't have options. They built because they had to. That hunger is India's greatest untapped advantage."
But something is shifting. The rise of no-code tools, AI copilots, and WhatsApp-native products is rewriting what it takes to build a startup in 2026. The infrastructure gap that used to protect incumbents is collapsing.
Rahul Vohra · Founder, Superhuman India · 12 min read

Featured Founder · May 2026
Priya Nair
Founder & CEO, SarvaBhasha AI

At 26, Priya Nair has built what many are calling India's most important AI company you've never heard of. SarvaBhasha AI enables businesses to deploy voice AI agents that seamlessly switch between English, Hindi, Tamil, and 8 other Indian languages — mid-sentence, without dropout.

Her customers include HDFC Bank, Jio, and 140 regional NBFCs. Monthly recurring revenue crossed ₹2.1Cr in April. She has never raised from a traditional VC — the company is funded by revenue and a ₹35L angel cheque from Girish Mathrubootham.

"The investors who told me my market was too niche are now cold-emailing me," she says, without any visible satisfaction. "I'm focused on building. The market will validate itself."
